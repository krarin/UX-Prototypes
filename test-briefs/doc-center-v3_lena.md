# Test Brief — DigitalQA Doc Center V3 / Lena

**File:** `doc-center/doc-center-skeleton/digital-qa-doc-center-v3.html`
**Persona:** lena-lead-hungry-closer
**Date:** 2026-06-15

---

## Primary task

Lena needs to quickly scan the document list, identify everything that needs action, and understand what she has to do next — without reading every document entry in detail.

---

## Steps (follow in this exact order)

1. Open the prototype. Note your immediate first impression — what draws your eye, what do you ignore.
2. Scan the document groups (Unkategorisiert, Persönliche Dokumente, Immobilienunterlagen). Do you understand the grouping without reading?
3. Find documents with a warning status. How many did you spot, and how quickly?
4. Click or hover on the first warning you see. What do you expect to happen? What actually happens?
5. Find the rejected documents (Exposé, Energieausweis). Note whether "rejected" is immediately distinct from "warning" at a glance.
6. Decide: what would you do next to move this case forward? Is that action obvious from the screen?

---

## Checklist (rate each 1–5 after completing the task)

Rate: 1 = broken or absent · 3 = works but causes hesitation · 5 = clear and effortless

| # | Item | Rating |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | At-a-glance: I can tell how many items need my attention without reading every row | |

---

## Focus areas

- Speed of status recognition: can Lena triage the list in under 10 seconds?
- Warning tooltip UX: does hovering feel natural, or does she miss that it exists?
- Visual hierarchy: does the layout guide her eye to what matters most?

---

## Out of scope

- Uploading or replacing documents
- Navigation to other sections of the application
- Mobile layout
